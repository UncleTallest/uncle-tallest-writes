# tallest-writes
*by Jerry Jackson (Uncle Tallest)*

---

I am a writer, philosopher, storyteller, and reluctant renaissance person operating somewhere in the overlap between campfire and lecture hall. I've been building frameworks, collecting questions, and telling stories for the better part of six decades. This is where I put them down in writing.

Nothing here is finished. Everything here is honest.

---

## What Lives Here

### [Philosophy](./philosophy/)
A body of work documenting how I think about existence, consciousness, belief, and the nature of reality. Anchored by the **Greater Me** framework — a personal cosmology built from the ground up over several decades, cross-referenced against comparative religion, physics, neuroscience, and the architecture of artificial intelligence.

Not a religion. Not self-help. Just rigorous thinking about hard questions, done in my own voice.

### [Fiction](./fiction/)
A trilogy in progress. More details when there's more to say. The voice Bible exists. The bones are solid. The words are coming.

### [Academic](./academic/)
Papers in progress on topics that earned a longer treatment than an essay allows. Same voice as everything else — I write academically the way I talk, which has been known to unsettle peer reviewers.

---

## On Voice

I write the way I talk. Pedantically, with occasional flashes of wry humor and a campfire storyteller's instinct for when to slow down and when to let something land without commentary. If you're expecting dry academic prose or performative humility, you're in the wrong repo.

I am opinionated. I am self-deprecating about being opinionated. I have spent six decades building an unusually broad base of knowledge across domains that don't typically talk to each other, and I think that breadth shows up in the work in ways worth preserving rather than sanding off.

---

## On Certainty

I don't claim certainty about much. What I do claim is internal consistency, honest methodology, and the willingness to say plainly when I'm working from the outside of something rather than the inside.

If I got something wrong — particularly in the comparative religion material — I want to know. Correction from people with fuller knowledge is better data, not a threat.
