# Greater Me — An Essay Collection
*by Jerry Jackson (Uncle Tallest)*

---

This is a collection of essays documenting a belief system I built over several decades. It's not a religion, not a self-help program, and not a prescription for anyone else. It's an honest accounting of what I believe, why it holds together, and where it came from.

The framework is called **Greater Me**. The short version: I exist (provable), I am part of something larger (coherent but unprovable), this reality is a context for something beyond itself (the lab), individual existence has purpose (the experiment), and whatever is essential returns to source when the experiment completes.

These essays explain the framework, address why it can't be proven (and why that's a feature), and show how it maps structurally across religious traditions and carefully constructed fictional cosmologies.

## Structure and Methodology

The comparative religion work uses a **three-criteria test**:

1. Reality as context for something larger (the lab)
2. Individual existence with purpose (the experiment)
3. Return to source

I'm testing for **structural fit**, not surface resemblance. The vocabulary differs across traditions. The cosmologies differ. The fit is functional, not literal.

Every tradition discussed here, I approached from outside or as a student. I'm not a practitioner of any of them. Correction from people with fuller knowledge is genuinely welcomed — not as a threat to the framework, but as better data.

## On Certainty and Humility

From *The Flawed Instrument*:

> "The instrument is flawed. It was always going to be. The map is not the territory, and the shard cannot observe the experiment from outside the experiment. Work with what you have. Know what it is. Build something that holds."

I can prove I exist. Everything else in this framework is built upward from that foundation with appropriate uncertainty. The Greater Me, the experimental nature of reality, the return to source — none of it is proven. All of it is coherent. None of it has broken under sustained pressure from philosophy, neuroscience, physics, or comparative religion.

That's not proof. It's coherence evidence. Inside the lab, with a flawed instrument, coherence is the honest metric.
