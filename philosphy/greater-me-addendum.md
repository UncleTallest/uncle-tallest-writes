## Ramtha — Early 1980s

Before I had language for what I believed, I encountered a set of teachings from an inter-dimensional being that a woman named J.Z. Knight claimed to channel. The entity was called Ramtha.

I was aware, even then, of the significant skepticism warranted by the channeling mechanism itself. I'm still aware of it. I'm setting it aside here not to endorse the source but to document the content — because the content mapped onto what I would later independently construct, and that's coherence evidence from my own timeline regardless of where the teachings originated.

The language Ramtha used was ahead of its cultural moment: frequencies, tuning in, consciousness as something that attunes to a larger field rather than something generated locally by biology. This reality as a stage for something larger. The individual as a temporary local expression of something more fundamental.

In the vocabulary I use now: the shard mechanic described in signal processing language. You tune in. You tune out. The frequency persists whether any particular receiver is active or not.

I was a teenager encountering this. I didn't have the comparative religion background yet, didn't have the physics vocabulary, didn't have the framework I would eventually build. But something in it landed as structurally familiar — like hearing a description of something I already knew in a language I hadn't encountered before.

Decades later, when I finally articulated Greater Me, I recognized the echo. The framework I built independently rhymed with teachings I'd encountered as a teenager from a source I couldn't fully credit. That's not evidence for Ramtha. It's evidence that the structure keeps appearing across wildly different contexts — which is exactly what the main document argues.

I include it here because honest biography requires it. This was in the mix. It mattered.
